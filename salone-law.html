Sierra Leone Legal Context

How open source licensing works under Sierra Leone law

🛡️

Cyber Security and Crime Act 2021

Sierra Leone's Cyber Security and Crime Act 2021 is the primary legislation governing digital activities. It addresses:

  • ✓ Cyber crimes — Unauthorized access, hacking, and digital fraud
  • ✓ Electronic evidence — Rules for digital evidence in court
  • ✓ Data protection — Framework for personal data handling
  • ✓ Critical infrastructure — Protection of essential digital systems

Key for creators: The Act provides the legal basis for digital transactions and can support open source licensing decisions when disputes arise.

📜

Open Source Under Sierra Leone Law

Open source licenses work in Sierra Leone through international copyright frameworks that Sierra Leone participates in:

  • • Berne Convention — Sierra Leone is a member, automatically granting copyright protection internationally
  • • TRIPS Agreement — WTO agreement Sierra Leone follows on IP rights
  • • WIPO — World Intellectual Property Organization membership

Using internationally-recognized licenses (MIT, GPL, CC) provides robust protection that aligns with these frameworks.

🌐

What is a Digital Public Good (DPG)?

A Digital Public Good is open source technology, data, or standards that are freely available and can improve people's lives. It's defined by:

Open Source

Source code is publicly available

Data

Collected information that benefits the public

Standards

Technical specifications for interoperability

Algorithms

Decision-making processes

Why DPG matters for Sierra Leone: DPGs can accelerate digital transformation while reducing costs and increasing transparency in government services.

Did You Know?

Sierra Leone launched its National Digital Transformation Policy in 2021, aiming to make Sierra Leone a digital hub in West Africa by 2030.

This policy supports digital public goods adoption and creates opportunities for Sierra Leonean developers and creators.

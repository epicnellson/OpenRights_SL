What is OpenRights SL?

OpenRights SL is a legal-tech compliance tool designed specifically for Sierra Leonean digital creators. It helps you understand Intellectual Property Rights (IPR) and choose the right open source license for your creative works—whether you're building software, writing content, or sharing digital creations.

The tool guides you through a simple questionnaire to recommend the most appropriate license for your needs, then generates professional documents you can use to protect your work.

🇸🇱

Why It Matters in Sierra Leone

Sierra Leone's digital economy is growing rapidly, but many creators lack awareness of IP protection. Software developers, content creators, and digital artists often release work without proper licensing, leaving them vulnerable to exploitation or losing control over how their creations are used.

OpenRights SL addresses this gap by making IP licensing accessible and understandable. Our tool empowers Sierra Leonean creators to protect their work while contributing to the global open source community.

The Licenses Explained

MIT License

The most permissive license. Others can use, modify, and even sell your code as long as they include your copyright notice. Best for: anyone who wants maximum adoption.

Apache 2.0

Similar to MIT but adds patent protection. Contributors grant you a license to any patents they include. Best for: projects where patent coverage matters.

GPL v3

Strong copyleft: if someone modifies your code and distributes it, they must share their changes under the same license. Best for: ensuring your work stays open source.

Creative Commons (BY & BY-NC)

For creative content (images, videos, writing). BY requires attribution. BY-NC adds non-commercial restriction. Best for: content creators who want to share but control usage.

⚖️

Legal Framework

OpenRights SL is designed within the context of Sierra Leone's evolving digital legal framework, particularly the Cyber Security and Crime Act 2021. This Act addresses cybercrimes, electronic fraud, and unauthorized access while also establishing frameworks for digital evidence and intellectual property in the digital space.

While Sierra Leone continues to develop specific IP legislation for digital works, using internationally recognized open source licenses provides robust protection that aligns with global standards and is recognized under international copyright treaties (Berne Convention, TRIPS Agreement).

Note: This tool provides guidance and generates documents for licensing purposes. For specific legal advice, consult a qualified legal professional in Sierra Leone.
